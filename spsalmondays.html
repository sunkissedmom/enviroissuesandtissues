Kitsap Salmon Tours - Washington State University

Organized by the Kitsap Extension of Washington State University, the Kitsap Salmon Tours program provides an engaging opportunity for the community to connect with salmon, a vital keystone species in the Pacific Northwest ecosystem. Typically hosted the first week of November, participants can learn about the life cycle of salmon, their importance to local wildlife, and the role they play in maintaining the health of our rivers and streams. The tours aim to foster a deeper understanding of these remarkable fish and promote conservation efforts to protect their natural habitats. This educational initiative not only enhances awareness but also encourages community involvement in preserving the region's rich aquatic heritage.

While we develop a richness around our understanding of the importance of this keystone species, we must not forget the indigenous knowledge and work that is aimed at restoring the salmon population.

The equation for building back...

While deepening our understanding of the vital role salmon play as a keystone species, we must honor and integrate the indigenous knowledge that has long guided efforts to restore these populations.

The path to rebuilding salmon habitats and populations cannot be undertaken without indigenous wisdom and having meaningful engagement within these communities. Their traditions, stewardship practices, and cultural insights offer invaluable lessons in sustainable coexistence with nature.
