Filucy Bay

The Filucy Bay Preserve, managed by the Great Peninsula Conservancy, spans 170 acres on the Key Peninsula in Washington and features over a mile of protected shoreline. This tidal estuary supports diverse ecosystems, including upland forests, salt marshes, and nearshore habitats for species like Coho salmon, ESA-listed Chinook salmon, black bears, and a wide variety of bird species. The area has historical significance as a First Nations fishing site and is of interest to archaeologists.

Restoration efforts at the preserve focus on invasive species removal and native plantings to enhance habitat quality and water filtration. Recent projects included planting 3,000 native trees and shrubs, supported by grants and community volunteer events.
